The differential is the component that allows your wheels to rotate at different speeds when turning — a critical part of your drivetrain. When it fails, you'll notice it. Signs of a Failing Differential or Drive Shaft

  • Whining, howling, or humming noise that changes with vehicle speed
  • Clunking or banging noise when accelerating or turning
  • Vibration felt through the floorboard at highway speeds
  • Difficulty turning, especially at low speeds
  • Leaking differential fluid (oily spots under the rear of your vehicle)
  • Grinding noise when changing gears or turning
  • Shuddering or shaking on acceleration

Differential Fluid Service

Many drivers overlook differential fluid, but it's just as important as engine oil. Contaminated or low differential fluid causes accelerated wear on gears and bearings, leading to expensive repairs. We recommend having your differential fluid inspected every 30,000 miles and replaced per your manufacturer's schedule. Our fluid service removes old, degraded fluid and fills your differential with the correct high-quality lubricant.
